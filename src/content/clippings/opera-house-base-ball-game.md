The whole advertisement is eleven words: "Opera House. Don't Forget the Base Ball Game To-Night. Admission, 10 Cents." No teams named, no time given. Madison, South Dakota evidently knew who was playing, and if you had to ask, the answer was be there anyway.

It ran on March 16, 1899, which explains the venue. Nobody in South Dakota is playing nine innings outdoors in March. Indoor base ball had come up through armories and gymnasiums in the 1890s, and where a town had no armory, the opera house had a stage, seats and a roof, and that was that. Brookhaven would still be at it [five years later on a ladies night](/clippings/indoor-base-ball-ladies-night/).

The scalloped border is the best part. The printer reached for the ornamental box normally reserved for the visiting Shakespeare company, because in this house, tonight, base ball was the show.
