Sunday, October 20, 1935, was settling day on the border. "Monterrey and the La Junta ball club of Nuevo Laredo will play today the two remaining games of their scheduled three-game series," the Laredo Times reported, and there was nothing casual about the stakes: "The two games today will once and for all decide supremacy between the two." Game one at 3:45 in the afternoon, game two "at night beginning at 8:30 o'clock."

Manager Erasmo Flores had his rotation set, "Bragaña pitching the afternoon game while either Cabal or Montalvo will perform in the nightcap." The afternoon assignment was the one to circle, because if Rossel got the call for Monterrey, history said to expect something special. "The last time these two played on the mound against each other, a hurling duel the like of which had never been seen here resulted." Bragaña won it, "but it was close and pitching honors were about equally divided with each fanning the same number of men and having about the same number of hits credited against each."

The paper was not shy about what a rematch would mean: "Should the two face each other again in any of the games today another battle of the century will result." Two battles of the century, ten days apart, is a good rate even for 1935.

Getting in was painless. "Popular prices are being charged for the games this afternoon and night with one peso being the charge in grandstand, fifty cents for ladies and children, and fifty cents in the bleachers." However supremacy came out, La Junta's October was only warming up. The same page announced [the Kansas City Monarchs were on their way](/clippings/monarch-ball-club-plays-junta-soon/), and [an ad three columns wide had Jimmy Foxx booked in right behind them](/clippings/american-league-all-stars-vs-la-junta/).
