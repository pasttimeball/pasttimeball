Twelve men, twelve big block Ls, and a headline that settles all arguments in advance: "Champion Laredo Oilers Of 1927."

The Laredo Times printed this photograph in August 1935, eight years after the fact, and the caption explains the nostalgia. "The first half champion Laredo Oiler ball club of 1927 one member of which will be seen in action Saturday and Sunday when The Wallace Engravers play La Junta from Nuevo Laredo. Chester 'Lefty' Webb who was a member of the 1927 Laredo team and who is shown first on the back row from left to right, will pitch at least one of the games for the Engravers. Chester Webb was well known here." A hometown arm coming back through town with a visiting club was worth a trip to the photo files, and honestly the photo files won the day.

The roster is the treasure. "Front row, left to right, Bart Green pitiher; Tex Westerzell, third base; Lefty Bowles, outfielder; Earl Courtney, outfielder; Fred Le Gar, second base. Back row: Chester Webb, pitcher, Buddy Lewis, shortstop; Honeyman first base; Haddow, pitcher; Kloza, catcher; Mule Watson, pitcher; Pete Arsatti, catcher." Bart Green's position is printed "pitiher," which we choose to read as a specialty all his own. And any staff carrying a Lefty, a Tex and a Mule at the same time was never going to lose the nickname battle, whatever happened on the field.

The opponent waiting for Webb and the Engravers was La Junta of Nuevo Laredo, a name that gets around this corner of the collection. Two months later that same club would be [settling supremacy with Monterrey](/clippings/games-start-at-345-and-830/) and entertaining [some very famous visitors from up north](/clippings/monarch-ball-club-plays-junta-soon/). Border baseball in 1935 kept everybody's calendar full.
