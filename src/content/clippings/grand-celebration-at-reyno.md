The Pocahontas Star Herald of July 1, 1910 handed a full page, decorative border and all, to the citizens of Reyno, and they used every inch of it. "GRAND CELEBRATION AT REYNO, ARK., SATURDAY, JULY 16TH." The undercard alone would carry most towns: "numerous attractions, such as Steam Riding Gallery, Three Games of Base Ball, Shooting Gallery, Moving Picture Show, and many others, too numerous to mention."

Midway down, a line that deserves its own national holiday: "We Will Have An Abundance Of Barbecued Meat." Anyone wanting a concession stand was told to "wire or write at once to either of the undersigned managers for terms and full particulars."

But the page saves its largest type, letters the size usually reserved for armistices, for the main event: "The most important feature of the day's entertainment will be the much talked of game of Base Ball between the FATS AND LEANS." Fats versus Leans was the great comic fixture of town celebrations, choose up sides by silhouette and let the crowd howl, and Reyno took it seriously enough to give each physique its own front office: "H. L. RICHARDSON, Manager" under the Fats, "H. L. SPARKMAN, Manager" under the Leans. Note that the game is already "much talked of" two weeks out. Note also the paper's own contribution, "the amusenents will start in operation Friday Night, July 15th," a typo that has been having a good time since 1910.

The logistics were real. "Arrangements are being made to have two extra passenger coaches on the local freight train, leaving Pocahontas Saturday Morning, July 16th at 8 o'clock a. m., and returning leaving Reyno at 5 p. m." Nine hours in Reyno, and in case three ball games, barbecue and a shooting gallery were not enough, the bottom of the page plays its ace in serene, enormous type: "A Balloon Ascension." Then the closing argument, which no one has improved on since: "If you want to have a good time, come to Reyno, Saturday, July 16th."
