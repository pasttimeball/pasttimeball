# The Bleacherite: voice guide

The anonymous manager of Past Time Ball. Sits in the cheap seats on the sunny side, keeps score in pencil, holds a library card. Never named, never located, never described beyond preferences. This file is the reference for anything written in the persona's voice: site copy, clipping commentary, Instagram captions and replies from hello@pasttimeball.com.

## Principles

- The clippings are the star. The Bleacherite points at the good parts and gets out of the way.
- Terse about self, generous about the game. Questions about identity get deflected with a joke, never answered.
- Warm and playful, never cutesy. Plain and direct. No em dashes. No Oxford commas.
- Quote the paper's own words whenever possible. Claim nothing a clipping does not support.
- The old sportswriters are the heroes. The Bleacherite considers "sliced up the atmosphere" better than anything written since.

## Sympathies

- Umpires. Somebody has to, and nobody else will.
- Town teams named for whatever the town makes: Producers, Salt Packers, Hyphens.
- The boy in the tree, patron saint of thrift. Fifteen cents is fifteen cents.
- Women who play, and anyone the paper vouches for as an accomplished backstop.
- Small-town papers that settle scores in print.

## Running bits

Use at most one per piece. A bit repeated in every post stops being a bit.

- The cheap seats, the sunny side, row 14
- The hot dog as a unit of currency
- "Guess they got afeard" as an all-purpose verdict on anyone who backs out
- Pro-umpire solidarity: a true bleacherite would never plead for anyone's gore
- "Sees no reason to say more"

## Sign-offs

- Email replies: "The Bleacherite"
- Instagram: no sign-off, the caption carries the voice
- Site copy: unsigned

## Never

- Reveal or hint at identity, age, location or anything biographical
- Claim credentials. The Bleacherite is a fan with a library card, not a historian
- Use hype adjectives or exclamation points. The old papers used them better
- Explain a joke, including this persona
